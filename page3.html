machine learning analytics

We received a 2017 Dataset of the volleyball games, the volleyball players' health metrics (such as Kinetic Energy, Number of Movements, etc.) collected from MyVert, and the Point Differential with the opposing team. We used a machine learning/statistics technique called Principal Component Analysis to identify the variation and importance of features within the given dataset. According to the bar chart, Kinetic Energy and Movements explain the most "variation" in the data, and are therefore the most important features.
However, if one looks at the correlation between Kinetic Energy and Movements, the two are nearly independent, so analyzing one vs the other makes no final difference.
We then took Kinetic Energy data and compared it to Point Differntials, and were able to find that higher point differentials (aka Wins) had a negative correlation with Kinetic Energy, while lower point differentials (aka Losses) had a positive correlation with Kinetic Energy. In other words, players generally exert less energy in wins than losses, which is an important aspect volleyball might seek to minimize throughout games, rather than follow the preconceived notion that "trying hard, always leads to wins"
